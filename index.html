Research

I am a Research Assistant in the Department of Electrical Communication Engineering at Indian Institute of Science. I am a Research Assistant in the Department of Electrical Communication Engineering at the Indian Institute of Science. I work on deep learning research focusing on representation learning and generative modeling. I am interested in exploring diffusion models, score-based generative modeling, and energy-based models (EBMs) with applications to medical imaging. My current work includes learning inductive biases (invariance and equivariance) for score-based generative modeling with a continuous time formulation using stochastic differential equations (SDEs). The convergence of diffusion models under the manifold hypothesis and variational diffusion models using a measure theoretical framework is one area I wish to explore keenly in the coming days. I aim to work on the following topics:

  • The expressivity of push-forward generative models under push-forward measures (eg: Poisson Flow generative models) using Lipschitz mappings.
  • A general analysis for incorporating invariance and equivariance symmetries for variance-exploding (VE) and variance-preserving (VP) SDEs in score-based generative modeling using Lie Algebra.
  • Automatic symmetry discovery for continuous image manifolds by utilizing the inherent data biases for low-resource settings.

My work has been presented at the leading machine learning conferences (NeurIPS and ICML), and includes a best paper award. Personally, I’m currently enthusiastic about our paper on learning what invariance should be used as an inductive bias for a dataset of interest.

See my Research Overview page for more details on my research interests.

About

Before starting at Imperial, I worked with Dr. James Hensman for two years as a machine learning researcher at Secondmind, a research-led startup aiming to solve a wide variety of decision making problems. I did my PhD in the Machine Learning Group at the University of Cambridge, working with Prof. Carl Rasmussen, and completing my thesis in 2017. I was funded by the EPSRC and awarded a Qualcomm Innovation Fellowship for my final year. During my PhD, I occasionally worked as a machine learning consultant, and I also spent a few months as a visiting researcher at Google in Mountain View, CA. I moved to the UK from the Netherlands for my undergraduate degree in Engineering.
